WisDOT: Majority of Wisconsinites ready for REAL ID enforcement

MADISON, Wis. -- Over 64% of Wisconsinites already have a REAL ID driver's license or identification card ahead of the upcoming travel enforcement, according to the Wisconsin Department of Transportation (WisDOT) Division of Motor Vehicles (DMV). The Transportation of Security Administration (TSA) will begin requiring passengers to show a REAL ID before boarding a domestic aircraft or visiting a federal building starting May 7. Also keep in mind that paper receipts of REAL IDs are not accepted by the TSA. The most common form of acceptable identification are ID cards and driver licenses.

Henry Vilas Zoo announces birth of 2 river otter pups, will debut at Party for the Planet

MADISON, Wis. -- The Henry Vilas Zoo recently welcomed two new members to its family. "Elva was an excellent mother with her first litter back in 2021 and is once again proving to be an attentive parent," Zoo Executive Director Ronda Schwetz said.
